Waste Sorting

Waste Seperation

Waste separation, also known as waste sorting or waste segregation, is the process of dividing waste into different categories.

Magnetic Seperation

A specialized method of waste separation that utilizes magnetic properties to separate ferrous and non-ferrous materials.

Waste Screening Systems

Screening is the most standard form of separating solid waste, depending on its size, by using one or more screening surfaces

Sensor-based

Sensor-based sorting has become more established in recent years, and it’s commonly used in the mining industry.

Robotic Sorting

Robotic recycling sorting uses artificial intelligence (AI) and robotics to sort plastics, so humans don’t have to. With advanced cameras and technology, these companies count on robots to sort recycling.

X-Ray Sorting

The application of X-rays has also changed every aspect of life, most notably in the mining industry. The application of X-ray separation technology has overturned the traditional methods.

Induction Sorting

An induction sorting system (ISS for short) is the perfect addition to magnetic sorting and eddy-current separation for recovering residual metals from a mix of materials and minerals.

Color Sorting

Color sorters (often called optical sorters, digital sorters, or electronic color sorters) are sorting machines used on the production lines in bulk food processing.

Near infra-red(NIR)

NIR sorting is the industry’s preferred plastics sorting technology because it can accurately identify the many different polymers already in use today.
